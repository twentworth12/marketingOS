# Case Study: Skyscanner's Incident Response Transformation

## Customer
**Skyscanner** - A B2C travel booking platform helping travelers plan and book trips with ease and confidence.

## Industry
Travel Technology / Travel Booking

## Challenge
Skyscanner's existing incident response setup using Jira and Slack was insufficient for their scaling needs. Key problems included:

- Lack of a comprehensive solution to replace and improve existing processes without requiring extensive custom development
- Difficulty managing both internal and customer-facing incidents with the same tools
- Poor incident communication and collaboration workflows
- No simplified way to track non-technical incidents across the organization

## Solution
Skyscanner adopted **incident.io** to address these gaps, gaining:

- An intuitive incident response workflow supporting all incident types
- A platform accessible to non-technical teams with guided workflows
- Automated processes to reduce response times
- Seamless integrations (including Jira server and Catalog)

## Results & Outcomes
- Improved incident communication through separate, organized channels
- Expanded incident management adoption across non-engineering teams (data platform, employee enablement, equipment management)
- Enhanced documentation and organizational learning from incidents
- Faster issue resolution and reduced unreported incidents

## Key Quotes

> "Having separate channels of communication is really important...keeping incidents that affect our customers separate from everything else is a huge workflow improvement."

> "One of the unexpected benefits...is that we've managed to get different groups within our organization to manage incidents."

> "The amount of regular calls and contact we have with the team is superb."
