# incident.io Case Study: Etsy

## Customer
**Etsy** – A global marketplace for unique and creative goods with 2,000+ employees across hybrid offices worldwide.

## Industry
E-commerce / Technology

## Challenges

### Lack of Visibility and Data
- Unable to accurately measure true time spent resolving incidents, only tracking from page-to-resolution without accounting for follow-up discussions
- Limited visibility into lower-severity, internally-facing incidents
- Insufficient data collection for a comprehensive learning-from-incidents program

### Communication Overlaps
- Managing multiple simultaneous incidents in a single channel created confusion
- Inability to distinguish which team member was responding to which incident
- Growth at scale made single incident channels unsustainable

### Incident Response Process Issues
- Manual, document-heavy incident lead procedures that distracted from actual incident management
- Lack of psychological safety and standardization around incident debriefs despite having a blameless culture foundation

## Solution
**incident.io** was selected as the incident management platform based on:

- Direct Slack integration
- Incident response data aggregation and insights
- Intuitive user interface requiring minimal onboarding
- Responsive customer support and development team
- Customizable Workflows for automation

## Implementation & Results

### Adoption
- VP of Infrastructure's teams began tracking incidents organization-wide
- Extended adoption across payments, member support, trust and safety, and security teams

### Key Outcomes
- Transformed visibility into incident data previously happening invisibly
- Automated 95% of incident lead procedures using Workflows
- Enabled comprehensive monthly reporting to leadership (VPs, directors, CTO)
- Reduced cognitive load on incident leads to focus on actual problem-solving

### Data Improvements
Leadership gained ability to track:
- Number and frequency of incidents by severity level
- Personnel involved in incidents
- Total time spent including discussions and remediation planning
- Downstream remediation work stemming from incidents
