# incident.io Case Study: WorkOS

## Customer
**WorkOS** - A developer platform company

## Industry
Enterprise software / Developer platforms

## Customer Profile
- **VP of Engineering:** Alon Levi
- **Company Size:** Started with ~10 engineers, growing at time of case study
- **Background:** Founded by engineers with experience at Google, Dropbox, and Quip

## Problem/Challenge

**Manual Incident Response Process**
- Relied on Slack-driven workflows with minimal automation
- Used runbook-style documents but lacked systematic automation
- Processes were ad-hoc, requiring manual division of responsibilities for investigation, communication, and resolution

**Scaling Issues**
- Onboarding new team members proved challenging due to hands-on nature of incident response
- Documentation overhead increased as company grew
- High mental burden on incident responders

**Process Inefficiencies**
- Manual tracking of post-incident actions
- Difficulty retrieving historical incident data for analysis and learning

## Solution
Adopted **incident.io** as their dedicated incident management platform, moving away from a build-vs-buy evaluation in favor of leveraging specialized expertise.

## Key Features Implemented

1. **Automated Workflows** - Centralized incident hub creation with connected resources
2. **Follow-ups Feature** - Track post-incident work with automatic synchronization to Linear (their issue tracker)
3. **Unified Interface** - Consistent incident channels accessible to technical and non-technical teams

## Results/Outcomes

- Eliminated manual overhead during incident response
- Increased incident declaration rates through automation confidence
- Expanded incident definition to include customer support issues and investigations
- Improved cross-functional team involvement, including non-technical staff
- Enhanced post-incident tracking and consistency

## Key Quotes

**Alon Levi, VP of Engineering:**

**On lowering response burden:**
"We wanted it to be more flexible. We wanted to lower the burden for the incident responder to communicate with folks."

**On automation impact:**
"Workflows are really impactful. Being able to have rules around what happens during incidents is really useful."

"More focused communication and a clearer beginning and end to incidents are a huge benefit."
