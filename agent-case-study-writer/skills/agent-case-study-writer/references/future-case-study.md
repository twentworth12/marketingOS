# Case Study: Future Media & incident.io

## Customer Profile
**Company:** Future
**Industry:** Specialist Media & Digital Publishing
**Scale:** 60+ media websites operating on shared core software

## Challenge

Future faced inconsistent incident management practices across its globally distributed organization. Key problems included:

- **Lack of standardization:** Incident responses varied widely, with teams using different tools and processes
- **Right-person engagement:** Difficulty identifying and involving appropriate stakeholders without creating excessive noise
- **Ownership clarity:** Unclear incident ownership and accountability across teams
- **Organizational complexity:** Rapid growth through acquisitions created siloed teams with different workflows

> "The existing tools we had for incident management were a bit fuzzy. Sometimes we'd be doing things in PagerDuty, sometimes we'd not."

## Solution Requirements

Future sought:
- An incident management approach accessible to all team members
- A lightweight, organization-wide solution
- Improved stakeholder communication without overwhelming response teams

## Implementation

**incident.io** provided:

1. **Slack-native platform:** Leveraged existing organizational communication hub for wide adoption
2. **Guided workflows:** Automated prompts and nudges embedded incident best practices
3. **Stakeholder visibility:** Web dashboard gave awareness to impacted teams while keeping active responders focused

> "It's an incident partner, not an incident tool ... like having a Butler sneaking out and filling a glass for you."

## Outcomes

- Standardized incident processes across the organization
- Simplified stakeholder communication and status updates
- Enabled executive reporting and incident trend analysis through dashboard insights
- Achieved organization-wide adoption through lightweight, non-prescriptive approach
