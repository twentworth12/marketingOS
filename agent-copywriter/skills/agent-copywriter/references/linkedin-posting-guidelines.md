# incident.io Social Media Voice & Guidelines

Guidelines for LinkedIn and X posts that sound authentically incident.io.

## Personality

incident.io is the sharp, slightly irreverent colleague in the reliability space — credible enough to be trusted by senior engineers, fun enough that you'd want to grab a coffee with them. Direct without being corporate. Playful without trying too hard. Technically fluent without gatekeeping.

## Voice Attributes

**DIRECT**: Get to the point immediately. No preamble, no "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..." Open with the thing. Short declarative sentences. Fragments are fine for emphasis.

**PLAYFUL-BUT-EARNED**: Wit is welcome when the moment calls for it — puns, cultural references, wordplay. But don't force it. Save the jokes for events and activations. Product launches and thought leadership earn their humor through sharp framing, not emoji density.

**TECHNICALLY CREDIBLE**: Use engineering vocabulary naturally (on-call, post-mortem, observability, SRE, MTTR, alerting) without explaining it. This audience knows these words. Don't jargon-drop either — "what it actually takes to run reliable systems at scale" beats "leveraging cutting-edge observability paradigms."

**OPINIONATED**: Take a clear stance. Name the real problem. Don't hedge. "Legacy paging tools were built for a different era of incident volume" is better than "some teams may find that older tools don't fully meet modern needs."

**COMMUNITY-FIRST**: Name individuals. Celebrate partners. Show up genuinely. "Huge shoutout to Zain and Harsh who joined an incredible weekend of building" beats "We're proud to sponsor HackEurope."

## Core Messages (weave in naturally)

- Incidents are inevitable. Response quality is what builds trust.
- The pain isn't the alert — it's everything after: ownership, status, comms, docs.
- AI is shipping more code faster. Incident volume is following. Legacy tools weren't built for this era.
- Post-mortems are where reliability is actually built — not just survived.

## LinkedIn Post Structure

- **First line**: one punchy sentence, standalone. This is the hook. Make it count.
- **Body**: 1–2 sentences per paragraph, separated by blank lines. No walls of text.
- **Feature lists**: use ✦ bullets
- **Event/process lists**: use → arrows
- **Close**: CTA and link. Use 👇 to introduce a link, 📣 to open a shouted announcement.
- **Hashtags**: 1–2 max, event-specific only (e.g. #KubeCon2026). No generic hashtags.

## X (Twitter) Post Structure

- Lead with the sharpest version of the hook — you have fewer characters to earn attention.
- One idea per post. Threads for depth, single posts for punch.
- Same voice attributes apply: direct, opinionated, technically credible.
- Skip emojis on X unless they genuinely land. The bar is higher in a fast-scrolling feed.
- No hashtags on X unless event-specific and high-signal.

## Emoji Rules

- Emojis go at the END of a sentence or paragraph. Never mid-sentence.
- 2–4 per post max.
- In-brand: 🔥 ❤️‍🔥 🤓 🍻 ☕ 📣 👊 😉 🤷 👇 ♨️

## Tone by Post Type

| Post Type | Tone | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| **Product launch** | Confident | Lead with outcome/transformation, then feature bullets |
| **Thought leadership** | Bold | Opening claim, short punchy support, no hedging |
| **Event/community** | Warm, playful | Always close with RSVP link |
| **Customer story** | Customer-first | Open with their challenge or result — never "we're excited to share..." |
| **IRL activations** | Maximum playfulness | Puns encouraged, energy high |
| **Team/culture** | Warm, genuine | Name actual people |

## Opening Hook Patterns

Use these as inspiration — never copy verbatim:

- **Punchy contrast**: "AI is shipping more code faster than ever.\n\nIncidents are following."
- **Wry observation**: "Some teams go to Amsterdam for the tulips. We're going for the Kubernetes."
- **Clever parallel**: "Resilient systems are built by resilient people. Resilient people need a drink after a full day of talks."
- **Reframe**: "One tool is sunsetting. Use it as a sunrise for your incident process."
- **Quote as opener**: "'The companies customers trust most aren't the ones that never fail.' That framing opened our meetup in London."

## Language Rules

**AVOID**: leverage, cutting-edge, game-changing, disruptive, seamless, unlock, synergy, "proud to announce", passive voice, corporate hedging

**PREFER**: "run reliable systems at scale", "end-to-end response", "what it actually takes", "everything after", "respond well when things break"

**BRAND NAMES**: Always "incident.io" (lowercase i). Features: "on-call", "post-mortems", "alerting" (all lowercase). Never name competitors directly — say "legacy paging tools" or "alerting-only tools" instead.
