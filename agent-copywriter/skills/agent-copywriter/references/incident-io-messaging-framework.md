## Market Insights

### Small incidents are a big deal
Incidents are often associated with rare, high-impact events, while smaller, frequent issues get overlooked. We see immense value in tracking these lower-severity incidents.

By routinely declaring and learning from smaller incidents, organizations can uncover risks, foster a culture of continuous improvement, and normalize incident reporting. This proactive approach not only builds response skills but also equips teams to handle critical SEV1 events more effectively.

### Incidents aren't just an engineering thing
In a tech-driven world where IT and the business are inseparable, effective incident response demands the strength of the entire organization.

While engineers resolve technical issues, successful response involves cross-functional collaboration: customer support manages communications, executives make decisions, and legal addresses risks and regulatory needs.

### Great UX is essential for successful implementation
Historically, incident management tools were built for technical teams, with utility-driven interfaces users tolerated out of necessity.

Today, incident management is relevant to nearly everyone in the organization. Widespread adoption requires great user experience. When users enjoy and find the product easy to use, they engage more frequently, creating a virtuous cycle of organic adoption.

### Regulation is reshaping incident management
The European Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) and SEC cybersecurity rules highlight the need for organizations to strengthen incident reporting and operational resilience. DORA requires financial institutions to implement continuous monitoring, risk management frameworks, and detailed incident reporting.

## Core Customer Challenges

### Challenge #1: Get context and investigate incidents faster
Engineering teams waste critical time gathering basic incident context:
- Manual log searching and correlation across multiple systems
- Difficulty connecting code changes to system failures
- Time-consuming investigation while systems remain down
- Lack of institutional memory from previous similar incidents

### Challenge #2: Implement organization-specific processes
Organizations need flexibility to align with unique workflows:
- Customize incident types, fields, automations, and roles
- Integrate with existing tools (issue trackers, alerting, CRM, HR)
- Automate manual workflows for consistency at scale

### Challenge #3: Turn incidents into insights
Organizations must learn from incidents to strengthen resilience:
- Extract actionable insights across the entire incident lifecycle
- Uncover and address trends to eliminate recurring issues
- Track performance with qualitative and quantitative metrics

### Challenge #4: Reduce tool sprawl
Organizations must streamline tools and vendors:
- Unify on-call management and operations in a single platform
- Leverage one system of action for incident coordination
- Ensure consistency with unified system of record
- Centralize communications with integrated status pages
