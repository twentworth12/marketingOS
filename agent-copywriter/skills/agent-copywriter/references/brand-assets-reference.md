## 01. Introduction: Who We Are

### Company Identity
We're incident.io, the all-in-one AI platform for on-call, incident response, and status pages - built for fast-moving teams. Our platform helps teams manage incidents quickly, clearly, and collaboratively so they can focus on solving problems, not juggling chaos.

We believe that incident management isn't just a process; it's a reflection of how a team communicates under pressure. That's why we build tools that feel calm, human, and empowering, because how you respond matters just as much as what went wrong.

### Mission Statement
**To help teams respond to incidents with confidence, clarity, and calm.**

We exist to make incident management effortless, connecting people, context, and communication in one place. Our mission is to turn chaos into clarity, empowering teams to learn, adapt, and grow stronger after every incident.

We're not here just to help teams fix problems faster - we're here to help them build better habits, nurture a culture of trust, and continuously improve how they work together.

### Brand Tagline
**"Move fast when you break things"**

### Company Values

| Value | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Raise the pace** | Pace is one of our superpowers. It builds momentum, closes deals and delights customers. Over time, most companies slow. We're always looking for ways to speed up. |
| **Find a way** | There are usually a million reasons why something can't be done, and often only a few why something can. We're driven by the belief that any problem can be solved if we approach it with positivity, grit, and a sprinkling of humor. |
| **Trust by default** | We default to giving each other the trust, space and autonomy to deliver our best work. With this high trust comes high expectations - we trust each other to hold ourselves accountable, do what we were hired to do, and do it really, really well. |
| **Make it magic** | A moment of unexpected surprise and delight leaves a long and lasting impression. These moments do not exist by chance, they are made. We believe that little extras, thoughtful details and extra effort make a huge difference. |
| **Win together** | We're in this together, with shared goals and a belief that our collective success trumps individual accolades. We cheer, we support, we celebrate, and sometimes we fail - but always as a team. |

## 02. Tone of Voice

### Our Voice (Consistent Foundation)
Our voice is our brand's personality - the way we show up in the world, no matter the channel or circumstance. It's steady, recognisable, and consistent. This is the foundation people trust.

**We sound like calm experts who know what they're doing, move quickly without shouting, and stay human even when things get messy.**

### Voice Traits

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Strong, not aggressive** | We move fast and lead with conviction, without shouting. |
| **Playful, with a wink** | Human and light where it counts, even under pressure. |
| **Reliable and trustworthy** | A calm, dependable partner when things go wrong. |
| **Clear and direct** | We speak plainly, cut fluff, and get to the point. |
| **Conversational and approachable** | Like a smart teammate in Slack, not a corporate manual. |

### Our Tone (Context-Adaptive)
Our tone is how our voice adapts to the moment. The personality stays the same, but the expression shifts depending on what the reader needs.

| Context | Tone Approach |
|---------|---------------|
| **During incidents** | Calm, steady, and reassuring. We keep people focused and reduce stress. |
| **Product/feature announcements** | Energetic, optimistic, and clear about the value. |
| **Docs and support content** | Straightforward, instructional, and helpful - no ego, no fluff. |
| **Outages or sensitive moments** | Human, empathetic, and gentle. Warmth takes priority over wit. |
| **Community or social content** | Light, friendly, and a touch playful. |

## 03. Logo System

### Primary Logo (Flame Mark + Wordmark)
The incident.io logo is the cornerstone of our brand identity. It represents who we are and should be used consistently across all touchpoints to ensure we're instantly recognisable and cohesive wherever we show up.

### Logo Color Variations

| Variation | Use Case | Background |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **Dark** | Primary color used on white and sand backgrounds | Light/white/sand surfaces |
| **Light** | Use across imagery and on dark coloured backgrounds | Dark backgrounds |
| **Mono** | For use on Alarmalade backgrounds | Alarmalade (#F25533) surfaces |

**Download Formats:** PNG, SVG

### The Icon (Flame Only)
The incident.io icon is a simplified version of our logo, designed for smaller spaces or where the full wordmark can't be used. It carries the same visual identity but in a more compact form.

**Important Usage Guidelines:**
- Because we're still building recognition in the icon, it should typically appear alongside or near the company name
- Examples: social media avatar, documents/pages where incident.io is clearly mentioned in the headline
- **The icon should never replace the full logo when introducing the brand or when clarity is important**

**Download Formats:** PNG, SVG

### Clear Space Rules
Our logo works best when it has room to breathe. Clear space ensures the logo stays legible, recognisable, and uncluttered.

**Minimum Clear Space:** Use the height of the flame to define the minimum clear space on all sides. This is our "safe zone," and nothing should intrude into it.

In tighter layouts, you can reduce the space slightly, but never to the point where the logo feels cramped or competing with other elements. **The goal is balance, not precision.**

### Logo Alignment (Optical vs Mathematical)
Our logo isn't mathematically perfect, and that's okay.

- The flame icon has more visual weight than the text, so the logo may appear slightly off-center if aligned strictly to its bounding box
- When placing the logo within grids, text areas, or margins, **use your eye - not just the ruler**
- Align it so it feels centred and harmonious, even if it isn't pixel-perfect on every edge
- When centring the logo in a layout, **nudge it up optically** so it appears visually balanced rather than mathematically symmetrical

### Logo Scale Guidelines

| Scale | Usage |
|-------|-------|
| **Small (1/3 width)** | Standard applications |
| **Medium (1/2 width)** | Moderate prominence |
| **Large (full width)** | Maximum impact |
| **Minimum size** | 120px wide (digital applications) |

For large-format designs (billboards, event banners), the standard scales may feel too big. In these cases, aim for roughly 1/5 or 1/7 of the layout width.

### Logo Don'ts (Absolute Rules)
- Don't break the clear space rules
- Don't overlay the logo over text
- Don't add gradients or effects to the logo
- Don't recolor the logo outside our palette
- Don't transform (stretch, distort, rotate) the logo
- Don't put a stroke on the logo

### Co-branding & Partnerships
When our logo appears alongside partner brands:

1. **Visual Balance:** Logos should be visually equal in weight - not necessarily the same size, but appearing balanced in scale and presence
2. **Alignment:** Align on a common baseline or vertical center, depending on the layout
3. **Line Weight:** Pay attention to line weight between logos; ensure it has rounded ends and is not too light

**Co-branding Layout Options:**
- **Horizontal:** Where there is roughly an even width to both logos
- **Stacked:** When the partnership logo is wider than the incident.io logo
- **Icons:** For when both brands have distinct icons (use sparingly)

## 04. Color Palette

### Primary Palette
Our color palette is clean, confident, and calm - a reflection of how we want incident.io to feel in any environment. It balances a dependable set of neutrals with distinctive accent colours that provide contrast, accessibility, and recognisability.

**The neutrals set the tone; Alarmalade makes the point.**

| Color Name | Hex Code | RGB | CMYK | Pantone | Usage |
|------------|----------|-----|------|---------|-------|
| **White** | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | 0, 0, 0, 0 | Paper White | Clean, confident foundation; backgrounds |
| **Alarmalade** | `#F25533` | 242, 85, 51 | 0, 75, 77, 02 | 2026 C | Primary accent; our burst of energy |
| **Charcoal** | `#161618` | 22, 22, 24 | 8, 8, 0, 90 | Neutral Black C | Contrast, clarity, accessibility |
| **Burgundy** | `#5A0A17` | 89, 9, 23 | 10, 95, 75, 58 | 7427 C | Secondary accent |

### Sand Family (Surface Colors)

| Color Name | Hex Code | RGB | CMYK | Pantone | Usage |
|------------|----------|-----|------|---------|-------|
| **Sand** | `#F8F5F0` | 248, 245, 240 | 0, 2, 4, 3 | 11-0602 | Light backgrounds, cards |
| **Light Sand** | `#F1EBE2` | 241, 235, 226 | 12, 10, 14, 0 | 7527 C | Subtle differentiation, depth |
| **Dark Sand** | `#E4D9C8` | 228, 217, 200 | 19, 20, 23, 0 | 7528 C | Borders, stronger contrast |

### Color Pairings by Context

**Product (Clarity & Focus First):**
- Primary pairing: White background with Black text
- Supporting tones: Light Sand and Sand add structure and depth
- Accent: Alarmalade for alerts, highlights, and action moments

**Marketing (More Room for Personality):**
- Primary pairing: Sand background with Black text (and sometimes Alarmalade for emphasis)
- Supporting tones: White, Dark Sand, and Alarmalade add depth and contrast for campaigns, hero blocks, and CTAs
- Accents: Burgundy and Pink bring extra vibrancy when we need lift and contrast

**Critical Rule: We do not use Black as a background.**

### Using Alarmalade
Alarmalade is the jolt in our otherwise calm palette - the moment of energy that makes people stop and look. It's our most expressive color, and because of that, it's used purposefully.

**When to Use It:**
- In product: as an accent color for alerts & emphasis
- In marketing: as a flat background for high-visibility moments (OOH or paid ads)
- In layouts: to create energy, contrast, or a clear point of focus

**When NOT to Use It:**
- Don't use Alarmalade as a default background in all designs - its power comes from contrast
- Avoid setting long text on Alarmalade; it's best for short headlines or highlight areas

**If everything is orange, nothing is.**

### Color Accessibility
Accessibility isn't a constraint, it's part of what makes our brand feel trustworthy and professional.

| Combination | Compliance |
|-------------|------------|
| Charcoal on White | WCAG AAA |
| Charcoal on Sand | WCAG AAA |
| White on Charcoal | WCAG AAA |
| White on Alarmalade | WCAG AA |
| Alarmalade on White | WCAG AA (large text only) |

**Guidelines:**
- Aim for WCAG AA or higher for text and interface elements
- Avoid using Alarmalade for long text - it's best as an accent or highlight
- When in doubt, test your combinations for clarity and legibility
- Maintain minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text

## 06. Design Systems

### Grid System
Our layouts are guided by a simple, flexible grid system using a **10-column/row grid** based on the shortest side of the frame. This gives us a flexible structure that adapts to any format.

**Key Principles:**
- Use consistent margins and padding across layouts for visual clarity
- You're not bound by the grid - push elements slightly outside for expression
- The best layouts feel balanced, not symmetrical

**Format Examples:**
- Square format: 10 columns x 10 rows
- Landscape format: 10 rows (shortest side), columns adapt

### Visual Foundations

**Shadows:**
Shadows give subtle depth. They help create hierarchy and guide the eye without shouting for attention.

- Use soft, diffused shadows that feel natural, not harsh or dramatic
- Standard styles: shadow-web-ui, shadow-md, shadow-lg, shadow-xl
- Avoid coloured or heavy shadows; they should always feel understated
- **Shadows should support legibility, not decoration. If you notice them before the content, they're too strong.**

**Rounded Corners:**
Rounded corners make our brand feel human and friendly - never cold or mechanical.

- Standard corner radius: 8-20px
- Keep corner radii consistent across UI and marketing elements
- Larger shapes or containers can use slightly bigger radii to stay proportional
- Avoid mixing multiple corner radii within one layout; consistency builds calm

**Roundness adds approachability. It's the quiet detail that keeps our brand feeling soft, not sharp.**

## 07. Showing Product in Design

When showing product in design, be intentional about what story you're telling. We showcase the product in different ways - sometimes functionally, showing it exactly as it is, and other times stylised, bringing out key elements or reworking the design for impact and playfulness.

### Product Display Spectrum

| Style | Usage | Best For |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Functional** | Real product UI for clarity and authenticity | Demos, docs, web sections that explain how we work |
| **Middle Ground** | Balanced between real and stylised | Feature highlights, presentations |
| **Stylised** | Simplified, cropped, or redesigned versions | Moments where we want to lead with emotion and impact |

## 08. Illustration & Art Direction

### Photography Guidelines
Our visual style is real, collaborative, and human. We show authentic moments, real people working together, not staged or overly polished setups.

**Photography Approach:**
- Candid, natural, and story-led
- Favor portraits or in-action shots over static "corporate" imagery
- Authentic, not stock-looking
- Technical environments (servers, code, dashboards)
- Real engineering teams in natural settings

**Color Treatment:**
- Cooler overall tones with subtle warmth
- A thread of orange (like a lamp, object, or accent) connects back to our brand

**Lighting:**
- Soft, balanced, and natural

## 09. Motion & Video Guidelines

> **📹 For comprehensive video production guidelines, see [video-brand-guidelines.md](video-brand-guidelines.md)**
> Complete guide covering typography specs, minimum text sizes, animation techniques, quality checklists, and platform-specific requirements for creating professional, on-brand video content.

### Motion Content Philosophy
Our motion content is designed to stand out across social, with product updates, recruitment posts and quick storytelling moments. Rather than full brand-production films, these are **punchy, high-impact assets** created primarily in **Figma to Jitter** and **Remotion**, optimized for clarity, rhythm and brand recall.

**Video Approach:** Cinematic and narrative-driven. Every frame should feel intentional, with clear storytelling. Professional and polished with playful energy through animation.

### Animation Principles

**Headlines:** Typically rise cleanly from the baseline

**Supporting Text:** Fading or sliding in using snappy, purposeful easing

**UI Elements/Graphics:** Appear with quick pops that are fun and playful

**Timing:** Steady and uncluttered - each beat has room to read before the next arrives

### End Frames
All videos should use consistent end frames, built from the same Jitter template. Our end frames for motion can be found in Jitter - the CTA copy can change depending on the video.

### Name & Job Title Animations (Recruitment/AMA Videos)
Name + job-title animations must follow one standard style for every recruitment or AMA video.

**Typography Specs for Name/Title Cards:**
- **Name:** STK Bureau Sans Bold
- **Job Title:** STK Bureau Serif Book
- **Line Spacing:** 110%
- **Tracking:** 3% (or -15 to -20 in Adobe)
- **Drop Shadow:** None, or minimal to make legible (avoid heavy shadows)

**Example Layout:**
```
James Sweeney
Enterprise Sales Team Lead
```

### Motion Style Summary
The result is motion that feels **clear, composed and instantly recognisable as incident.io.**

## Quick Reference for Image/Video Generation

### Essential Brand Elements Checklist
1. **Primary Accent Color**: Alarmalade #F25533
2. **Text Color**: Charcoal #161618
3. **Background Options**: White, Sand #F8F5F0, or Light Sand #F1EBE2 (never Black)
4. **Headline Font**: STK Bureau Serif Book (letter spacing -3%, line height 90%)
5. **Body Font**: STK Bureau Sans Book (letter spacing 0%, line height 120%)
6. **Technical Font**: Geist Mono Medium
7. **Accent Font**: Kalam Regular (handwritten touches - use sparingly)
8. **Corner Radius**: 8-20px
9. **Always use**: Sentence case

### Brand Personality in Visuals
- Calm, confident, and composed
- Technical but approachable
- Strong without being aggressive
- Warm sand tones, not cold steel grays
- Alarmalade energy conveys urgency and action
- Clean layouts with generous white space
- Human and friendly, never cold or mechanical

### Logo Placement Best Practices
- Top-left corner for headers
- Bottom-right for social/marketing materials
- Centered for splash screens or loading states
- Always maintain minimum clear space (height of flame)
- Use optical alignment, not mathematical

### Video/Motion Checklist
- Use Jitter templates for end frames
- Headlines rise from baseline
- Supporting text fades/slides with snappy easing
- UI elements pop in playfully
- Steady timing with room to read
- Consistent name/title animation style
- No heavy drop shadows
