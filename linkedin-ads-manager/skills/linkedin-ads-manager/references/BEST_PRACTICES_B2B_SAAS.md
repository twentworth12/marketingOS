# LinkedIn Ads Best Practices for B2B SaaS

**Strategic guidance for full-funnel LinkedIn advertising programs**
**Last updated: February 4, 2026**

---

## Overview

LinkedIn Ads deliver strong ROI for B2B SaaS when you build a full-funnel program, use precision targeting, and optimize systematically. This guide provides practical best practices drawn from 2025-2026 benchmarks and proven case studies.

**When LinkedIn works best:**
- B2B SaaS with high ACV (typically $10,000+ annually)
- Clear ICP with buying committees of 3+ stakeholders
- CPL of $125-$300 is economically viable
- 3-6 month sales cycles
- Average ROAS: 113% (vs. Google 98%, Meta 104%)

## 1. Strategy & Funnel Architecture

### Define Clear Objectives
- **Tie goals to revenue:** Pipeline from ICP accounts, SQL volume, demo bookings, or influenced revenue
- **Focus quarterly:** 1-2 core objectives per quarter (e.g., "pipeline from ICP accounts," "demo requests")
- **Commit to test windows:** Minimum 3-6 months of consistent spend before judging the channel
  - LinkedIn's algorithm requires time to optimize
  - B2B buying cycles are long
  - Most experts recommend at least 6 months for valid assessment

### Build a Three-Stage Funnel

**Stage 1: Awareness (20-30% budget)**
- **Goal:** Impressions and reach into cold ICP
- **Content types:** Problem-education, benchmarks, industry reports
- **Offers:** "How we cut MTTR by 43% in 90 days" teardowns, benchmarks reports, teardown carousels
- **Formats:** Video, carousels, single image

**Stage 2: Consideration (40-50% budget)**
- **Goal:** Engagement and content consumption
- **Content types:** Case studies, comparison guides, webinars, product use-case breakdowns
- **Offers:** Customer success stories, comparison guides, educational webinars
- **Formats:** Lead gen forms, thought leader ads, carousels

**Stage 3: Decision (20-30% budget)**
- **Goal:** Conversions (demos, trials, meetings)
- **Content types:** Direct CTAs, ROI calculators, product demos
- **Offers:** Demo bookings, free trials, ROI calculators, "see how we replaced X tool"
- **Targeting:** Retargeting high-intent visitors (pricing/demo pages)
- **Formats:** Lead gen forms, conversation ads

### Budget Allocation Example (from $904K annual case study)

**60% Cold Prospecting/Demand Gen:**
- 10% awareness (thought leadership, brand content)
- 25% consideration (educational content, case studies)
- 25% bottom-funnel (direct conversion offers)

**40% Retargeting:**
- 30% web visitors pre-lead (nurture warm traffic)
- 10% pipeline/post-lead (account-based acceleration)

## 2. Targeting: Precision Over Scale

### ICP-First Approach

**Start with firmographics:**
1. Company size (employee count)
2. Industry vertical
3. Geography
4. Annual revenue (if available)

**Layer buyer criteria:**
1. Job function (not just titles)
2. Seniority level
3. Skills (technical competencies)

**Best practice combo:**
- Company targeting + job function + seniority level
- Example: "Software companies, 200-2,000 employees, Engineering + IT functions, Director+"

**Avoid over-reliance on job titles:**
- Titles vary wildly across organizations
- Fragments your audience unnecessarily
- Use function + seniority instead for broader reach

### Build Warm Audiences Immediately

**Critical first steps:**
1. **Install Insight Tag** on your website (foundational)
2. **Create retargeting audiences:**
   - Website visitors (all pages)
   - Pricing page visitors (high intent)
   - Product demo page visitors (very high intent)
   - Video viewers (engagement signal)
   - Post engagers (awareness signal)
3. **Upload CRM/account lists** for ABM targeting
4. **Create lookalike audiences** from high-value customers (when you have 1,000+ records)

### ABM Targeting for Enterprise SaaS

**Account-based approach:**
1. Upload 1,000+ target accounts from CRM/Clay
2. Layer buyer functions: VP Eng, Head of DevOps, CRO, Head of Security
3. Create account-specific creative or offers when possible (for top 50-100 accounts)
4. Use conversation ads for high-touch outreach to key accounts

**Example ABM structure:**
- List: Top 500 target accounts
- Functions: Engineering, IT, Security
- Seniority: Director+
- Skills: DevOps, SRE, Incident Management, PagerDuty, Kubernetes
- Budget: $50-100/day per account tier

### Critical Settings

**Disable Audience Expansion:**
- LinkedIn's expansion often adds low-quality traffic
- Keep budget focused on precise ICP
- Only use expansion for top-of-funnel awareness campaigns with large budgets

**Use Exclusions:**
- Exclude irrelevant job functions (HR, Finance, Sales - unless they're your buyer)
- Exclude irrelevant geographies
- Exclude existing customers (create suppression list)
- Exclude unqualified company sizes

## 3. Ad Formats & Offers

### Format Performance Guide

| Format | Best Use Case | Typical Performance | When to Use |
|--------|--------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Single Image + Lead Gen Form** | Lead capture, gated assets | CPL $75-$150; form conv. 6-10% | Workhorse for middle/bottom funnel |
| **Carousel** | Multi-feature storytelling, product tours | ~2× CTR vs single image | Product education, use case sequences |
| **Video** | Engagement, brand awareness, demos | 5× engagement vs static; 25-40% completion on 15s | Top-of-funnel awareness, product demos |
| **Thought Leader Ads** | Trust-building, mid-funnel, SME content | 1.7× CTR, up to 40% lower CPL | CEO/founder insights, expert content |
| **Conversation Ads** | ABM, event invites, high-touch outreach | 3% open-to-click; ~$0.50/send; 4× engagement on target accounts | Enterprise ABM, exclusive events |

### Lead Gen Forms vs. Landing Pages

**Lead Gen Forms (On-Platform):**
- ✅ Convert 5× better than landing pages
- ✅ Frictionless experience (pre-filled data)
- ✅ Lower cost per lead
- ⚠️ May produce lower-intent leads
- **Best for:** Gated content, reports, webinars

**Landing Pages (Off-Platform):**
- ✅ Higher-intent leads (extra qualification step)
- ✅ More context and brand storytelling
- ✅ Better tracking/attribution
- ⚠️ 25-50% higher cost per lead
- **Best for:** Demo requests, free trials, high-commitment offers

**Recommendation:** Test both. Optimize for SQL rate, not raw CPL.

### Offer Ideas That Convert in B2B SaaS

**Educational (Top/Middle Funnel):**
- Industry benchmarks report tailored to ICP
- "State of [Industry]" research with proprietary data
- ROI calculator or assessment tool
- Exclusive workshop or masterclass
- Problem-breakdown teardown: "How [Customer] cut [Metric] by X% in Y days"

**Conversion (Bottom Funnel):**
- Live product demo or working session
- Free trial with white-glove onboarding
- ROI analysis/business case builder
- Competitive comparison guide
- "See how we replaced [Competitor] in 30 days"

**Event-Based:**
- Invitation to exclusive roundtable (for enterprise prospects)
- Early access to product beta
- Customer advisory board invite
- Live incident postmortem breakdown session

## 4. Creative & Messaging

### The Hook Formula (80% of the Work)

**Your first line must call out:**
- **Role:** "SRE teams drowning in noisy alerts?"
- **Problem:** "Incident response without pager chaos"
- **Desired outcome:** "Cut MTTR 43% in 90 days"

**Examples of strong hooks:**
- "DevOps teams waste 12 hours/week on false alerts. Here's how [Customer] fixed it."
- "Your incident response is probably broken. Here's the $2M problem you're not seeing."
- "We analyzed 10,000 incidents. Most teams make these 3 mistakes."

### Focus on Pain + Outcome, Not Features

**❌ Feature-focused:**
"Our platform has automated workflows, customizable dashboards, and real-time alerts."

**✅ Outcome-focused:**
"Cut mean time to resolution from 4 hours to 37 minutes. No more 2am pages for false alarms."

**Use customer proof:**
- Specific numbers: "43% reduction in MTTR"
- Named customers (with permission): "Netflix uses our platform"
- Quantified outcomes: "Eliminated $840K in lost revenue from downtime"

### Keep Creative Native to LinkedIn

**What works:**
- Simple layouts with clear hierarchy
- Product UI screenshots (show, don't tell)
- Founder/SME faces (builds trust)
- Authentic language (conversational, not corporate)
- User-generated content (customer testimonials)

**What doesn't work:**
- Polished "banner ads" (ignored as ads)
- Stock photos (low trust)
- Heavy text overlays (hard to read on mobile)
- Corporate jargon (immediate skip)

### Always Include One Clear CTA

**Match CTA to funnel stage:**
- **Awareness:** "Read breakdown," "Download report," "Watch video"
- **Consideration:** "See how it works," "Compare solutions," "Join webinar"
- **Decision:** "Book demo," "Start free trial," "Get ROI analysis"

**CTA best practices:**
- Use action verbs: "Grab," "Download," "Book," "Start"
- Create urgency: "Join 50 SRE leaders," "Limited spots"
- Remove friction: "No credit card required," "15-minute setup"

### Hyper-Target Creatives to Qualify Leads

**Explicit persona callouts:**
- Visual: Show persona using product ("CFO reviewing dashboard")
- Text: Call out role in headline ("ChatGPT for CROs")
- Offer: Role-specific value prop ("CFO-specific financial modeling")

**Why this works:**
- Filters clickers (self-qualifying)
- Improves lead quality (role-matched)
- Reduces wasted clicks (wrong personas skip)
- Increases conversion rates (relevance)

### Refresh Creative Every 14 Days

**Ad fatigue timeline:**
- Days 1-7: Strong performance
- Days 8-14: CTR decline begins
- Days 15+: Significant CTR drop (30-50%)

**Refresh strategy:**
- Rotate 3-4 creative variants
- Test new hooks/visuals every 2 weeks
- Pause ads when CTR drops below 0.45%
- Keep winning concepts, refresh execution

## 5. Budget, Bidding & Optimization

### Budget Allocation

**Overall digital ad budget:**
- Allocate 25-30% to LinkedIn for B2B SaaS
- Example: $100K/month digital → $25-30K LinkedIn

**Funnel budget split (recommended):**
| Stage | Budget % | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Awareness | 20-30% | Cold prospecting, brand awareness |
| Consideration | 40-50% | Educational content, nurture |
| Decision | 20-30% | Conversion offers, retargeting |

**Campaign-level budgets:**
- Awareness campaigns: $50-100/day
- Consideration campaigns: $75-150/day
- Decision/retargeting campaigns: $25-75/day
- ABM account campaigns: $50-100/day per account tier

**Never go below $15/day per campaign:**
- Won't get enough volume for optimization
- LinkedIn algorithm needs minimum spend to learn
- Campaigns under $15/day rarely deliver results

### Bidding Strategy Evolution

**Phase 1: Learning (Weeks 1-2)**
- Use **cost-cap bidding** to establish baseline
- Set cap at 1.5× target CPA
- Let LinkedIn optimize delivery
- Monitor CTR and conversion rate

**Phase 2: Optimization (Weeks 3-4)**
- Switch to **manual bidding** with day-parting
- Set daily budgets 30% higher than target spend
- Use aggressive bid caps to improve efficiency
- Pause ads when CTR drops below 0.45%

**Phase 3: Scaling (Week 5+)**
- Use **manual bidding** for proven campaigns
- Test increased budgets on top performers
- Duplicate winning campaigns with slight variations
- Use automated rules for bid adjustments

### Implement CAPI (Conversions API)

**Impact of CAPI:**
- ~20% lower CPA (cost per acquisition)
- 18-22% better attribution accuracy
- Optimizes toward actual pipeline events (not just clicks)
- Reduces dependency on cookies/pixels

**How to implement:**
1. Set up server-side tracking (Segment, Rudderstack, or custom)
2. Send conversion events directly to LinkedIn API
3. Match with Insight Tag data for validation
4. Optimize campaigns toward CAPI conversions (SQLs, pipeline)

### Track What Matters

**Essential conversion tracking:**
1. **Insight Tag installation** (baseline requirement)
2. **Conversion tracking setup:**
   - Form submissions (lead)
   - Demo bookings (high intent)
   - Trial signups (conversion)
   - SQLs (pipeline)
   - Closed-won deals (revenue attribution)
3. **UTM parameter structure:**
   - utm_source=linkedin
   - utm_medium=paid-social
   - utm_campaign={campaign_name}
   - utm_content={ad_id}
   - utm_term={audience_segment}

**Optimize toward:**
- SQLs (not raw leads) → Better quality signal
- Pipeline influenced → Revenue impact
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) → True unit economics

### Always-Be-Testing Framework

**What to test (one variable at a time):**
- Hooks (first line variations)
- Visuals (product UI vs. founder face vs. diagram)
- Offers (report vs. webinar vs. demo)
- CTAs (action verb variations)
- Formats (single image vs. carousel vs. video)
- Audiences (job function vs. skills vs. account lists)

**Test discipline:**
- Change ONE variable at a time
- Give each test minimum 1,000 impressions
- Statistical significance: 95% confidence
- Document learnings in playbook

**When to pause ads:**
- CTR drops below 0.45% (ad fatigue)
- CPC exceeds benchmarks by 15%+ (inefficiency)
- Zero conversions after 3× normal CPL in spend
- Creative refresh needed after 14 days

### Monitor Frequency

**Target frequency:**
- 2-3 impressions per user per week (awareness)
- 3-5 impressions per user per week (consideration)
- 5-8 impressions per user per week (retargeting)

**Watch for frequency burnout:**
- Frequency >10 per week → CTR declines sharply
- CPC increases as audience fatigues
- Negative brand perception risk

**How to manage frequency:**
- Set frequency caps in campaign settings
- Rotate creative every 14 days
- Expand audience if frequency exceeds 8/week
- Use exclusion lists to prevent over-targeting

## 7. Operational Best Practices

### 14-Day Creative Refresh Cycle

**Why 14 days:**
- CTR typically declines after 2 weeks of exposure
- Audience fatigue sets in
- Performance drops 30-50% without refresh

**Refresh workflow:**
1. **Day 1-7:** Monitor performance, collect learnings
2. **Day 8-14:** Identify winners/losers, plan next variants
3. **Day 14:** Pause fatigued ads, launch new creative
4. **Day 15+:** New cycle begins with refreshed assets

**What to refresh:**
- New hook/headline (test different pain points)
- New visual (rotate product UI, founder, diagram)
- New offer (if bottom-funnel performance is weak)
- Same concept, different execution

### Use Exclusions for Focus

**Exclude to concentrate budget:**
1. **Irrelevant job functions:**
   - If selling to Engineering, exclude: HR, Finance, Legal, Marketing
   - Exception: If multi-stakeholder deal, target decision-makers too
2. **Irrelevant geographies:**
   - If US/Canada only, exclude EMEA, APAC
   - Saves budget from low-intent international clicks
3. **Existing customers:**
   - Create suppression list from CRM
   - Upload as exclusion audience
   - Prevents wasted spend on existing accounts
4. **Unqualified company sizes:**
   - If targeting 200-2,000 employees, exclude <50 and >5,000
   - Focuses budget on ideal customer profile

### Leverage Thought Leader Ads

**What are thought leader ads:**
- Sponsored posts from individual profiles (not company page)
- Appear in news feed as personal content
- Can be from founder, SME, or even customers

**Why they work:**
- 1.7× higher CTR than standard sponsored content
- Up to 40% lower CPL
- Higher trust (personal voice vs. corporate)
- Better engagement (comments, shares)

**Best practices:**
1. **Use founder/CEO content** for trust and authenticity
2. **Share customer success stories** from customer profiles (with permission)
3. **Post educational content** from internal SMEs (engineers, product leaders)
4. **Avoid hard sales pitches** (focus on insights, not product features)

**Content ideas:**
- Founder sharing company learnings: "3 mistakes we made scaling to 1,000 customers"
- Customer praising your product: "How [Your Company] helped us reduce MTTR 43%"
- SME sharing industry insights: "The incident response playbook we use at [Your Company]"

### Integrate LinkedIn with Multichannel Nurture

**Cross-platform retargeting:**
1. **LinkedIn → Google Display:**
   - Retarget LinkedIn engagers on Display network
   - Lower cost, maintains brand recall
2. **LinkedIn → Meta:**
   - Retarget on Facebook/Instagram with different messaging
   - Test creative variations at lower CPMs
3. **LinkedIn → Reddit:**
   - Target communities aligned with ICP
   - Highly engaged technical audiences (DevOps, SRE)

**Email nurture integration:**
1. Capture leads via LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms
2. Sync to CRM/ESP immediately
3. Launch automated email nurture sequence
4. Retarget email openers on LinkedIn with next-stage offers

### Self-Reported Attribution + UTM Tracking

**Why self-reported attribution matters:**
- Cookies are increasingly unreliable
- Multi-touch journeys hard to track
- Prospects often research offline before converting

**How to implement:**
1. **Add field to all forms:** "How did you hear about us?"
   - Options: LinkedIn ad, Google search, Referral, Event, etc.
2. **Track UTMs in CRM:** Tag all conversions with source/medium/campaign
3. **Weekly attribution report:** Compare self-reported vs. UTM vs. platform attribution
4. **Pipeline influence analysis:** How many deals have LinkedIn touchpoints?

**Example attribution insights:**
- 40% of SQLs self-report LinkedIn as source
- But only 20% have last-touch LinkedIn attribution
- LinkedIn influences 60% of pipeline (multi-touch)
- → Proves incremental value beyond last-click

### Contact Your LinkedIn Rep Monthly

**What to request:**
1. **Audience penetration reports:**
   - How many unique users reached in target segment?
   - What % of your ICP have you touched?
2. **Competitive benchmarks:**
   - How does your performance compare to similar advertisers?
   - Industry-specific CTR/CPC/CPL data
3. **Beta feature access:**
   - Predictive audiences (2026 feature)
   - New ad formats or placement options
4. **Account-level optimization:**
   - Review creative performance across campaigns
   - Targeting recommendations based on your ICP

## 8. Key Trends Reshaping LinkedIn Ads (2025-2026)

### Budget Migration to LinkedIn

**Trend:** 39% of B2B marketers increasing LinkedIn spend in 2026
- LinkedIn ROAS averages **113%** (vs. Google 98%, Meta 104%)
- Better attribution and higher-quality leads driving shift
- Meta/Google facing saturation; LinkedIn still has headroom

**What this means:**
- Expect CPMs to rise 10-15% year-over-year
- Competition increasing in crowded verticals (FinTech, MarTech)
- Early movers in emerging categories (AI tools, DevOps) enjoy lower costs

### Video Expansion (28% of Impressions)

**Trend:** Video ads now 28% of LinkedIn impressions (up from 17% in 2024)
- Short-form video (<15 seconds) delivering 5× engagement vs. static
- 25-40% completion rates on 15-second spots
- Video viewers are 3× more likely to convert than static ad viewers

**What to do:**
- Develop native LinkedIn video (not repurposed YouTube/Meta content)
- Keep videos <15 seconds (sweet spot for completion rate)
- Use captions (85% watch without sound)
- Focus on product demos and customer testimonials

**Video content ideas:**
- 10-second product UI walkthrough
- 12-second customer testimonial clip
- 8-second problem/solution format
- 15-second founder insight or hot take

### CAPI Adoption Accelerating

**Trend:** Server-side tracking via Conversions API becoming table stakes
- ~20% lower CPA for advertisers using CAPI
- 18-22% better attribution accuracy
- Reduces dependency on cookies (privacy-compliant)

**Implementation priority:**
1. **Q1 2026:** Set up CAPI infrastructure (highest ROI improvement)
2. **Q2 2026:** Optimize campaigns toward CAPI conversions (SQLs, pipeline)
3. **Q3 2026:** Fully migrate off pixel-only tracking

**Tech stack requirements:**
- Customer data platform (Segment, Rudderstack)
- Server-side tracking (not just Insight Tag)
- CRM integration for closed-loop attribution

### Quality Score Influence Growing

**Trend:** Campaigns with CTR >0.7% enjoy ~15% lower CPCs
- LinkedIn's algorithm rewards engagement over bid amount
- High-quality ads (relevant, engaging) get preferential delivery
- Poor-performing ads face rising costs (negative spiral)

**How to optimize for Quality Score:**
1. **CTR is king:** Focus on hooks and relevance (aim for >0.6%)
2. **Engagement signals:** Comments, shares, likes improve score
3. **Conversion rate:** Ads that convert get cheaper delivery
4. **Relevance:** Tight audience-message fit improves performance

**Practical implications:**
- Better to spend $100/day on high-CTR ad than $200/day on low-CTR ad
- Creative quality matters more than budget size
- Pause ads below 0.45% CTR immediately (hurts account quality)

### Predictive Audiences (New 2026 Feature)

**What it is:**
- LinkedIn's AI-driven modeling to enhance targeting
- Analyzes conversion patterns to find similar prospects
- Similar to Facebook Lookalike Audiences but B2B-optimized

**How to prepare:**
1. **Build conversion history:** Need 100+ conversions for model training
2. **Track SQLs and pipeline** (not just leads) for higher-quality models
3. **Implement CAPI:** Powers the data that trains predictive models

**Expected impact:**
- 20-30% lower CPA on predictive audiences
- Broader reach without sacrificing quality
- Requires 6-12 months of conversion data for best results

### Thought Leader Ads = 2026 Game-Changer

**Why it's the highest-performing format:**
- 1.7× CTR vs. standard sponsored content
- Up to 40% lower CPL
- Personal voice (founder, SME) builds trust faster than corporate messaging
- Engagement (comments, shares) boosts algorithmic distribution

**How to leverage:**
1. **Founder content:** Share company learnings, behind-the-scenes insights
2. **Customer testimonials:** Sponsor posts from happy customers (with permission)
3. **SME insights:** Engineers, product leaders sharing technical expertise
4. **Avoid sales pitches:** Focus on education, insights, authenticity

**Competitive advantage:**
- Most B2B advertisers still use corporate page posts
- Early adopters of thought leader ads see outsized returns
- Limited by authentic content creation (can't fake it)

## 10. When LinkedIn Ads Are Worth It

### Strong Fit Indicators

✅ **Yes, invest in LinkedIn if:**
- B2B SaaS with **ACV ≥$10,000** annually
- **Clear ICP** with identifiable job titles/functions
- **Buying committees of 3+ stakeholders** (LinkedIn reaches all personas)
- **3-6 month sales cycle** (enough time for multi-touch nurture)
- **CPL of $125-$300 is economically viable** given your unit economics
- **You can commit to 3-6 months** of consistent spend (minimum test window)
- **You have resources** for creative production and campaign management

### Weak Fit Indicators

⚠️ **Reconsider or delay LinkedIn if:**
- **ACV <$5,000** (economics don't support $150+ CPL)
- **Product-led growth** motion (self-serve, no sales team) — use Google, Reddit instead
- **Consumer product** (B2C) — LinkedIn is B2B-first
- **Very broad ICP** (small businesses, solopreneurs) — LinkedIn targeting strength is wasted
- **Tight budget** (<$5K/month) — not enough volume for optimization
- **Short-term goals** (<90 days) — LinkedIn needs time to optimize

### ROI Reality Check

**Average B2B SaaS economics on LinkedIn (2026):**
- CPL: $125 (median for landing page conversions)
- Lead → SQL rate: 15-25%
- SQL → Opportunity: 30-40%
- Opportunity → Close: 20-30%
- **Blended CAC from LinkedIn:** $2,000-$4,000

**Is this viable for your business?**
- ACV $10K → 2.5-5× LTV:CAC (potentially viable)
- ACV $25K → 6-12× LTV:CAC (strong economics)
- ACV $50K+ → 12-25× LTV:CAC (excellent fit)

### Alternative Channels if LinkedIn Isn't Right

**If LinkedIn economics don't work:**

## 12. Summary: The LinkedIn Ads Playbook

### Must-Dos (Non-Negotiable)

1. ✅ **Install Insight Tag** and conversion tracking (week 1)
2. ✅ **Commit to 3-6 months** minimum (don't judge before 90 days)
3. ✅ **Build three-stage funnel** (awareness, consideration, decision)
4. ✅ **Start with $150/day minimum** ($50/day per campaign × 3 campaigns)
5. ✅ **ICP-first targeting** (firmographics → function → seniority)
6. ✅ **Skills-based targeting** (not just job titles) for broader reach
7. ✅ **Lead gen forms** for middle funnel, landing pages for high intent
8. ✅ **14-day creative refresh** to combat ad fatigue
9. ✅ **Track SQLs and pipeline** (not just leads) for true attribution
10. ✅ **Implement CAPI** within 90 days for 20% better performance

### Best Practices (Competitive Advantage)

1. 🎯 **Use thought leader ads** (1.7× CTR, 40% lower CPL)
2. 🎯 **Hyper-target creatives** to filter audience (improves lead quality)
3. 🎯 **Retarget across channels** (LinkedIn → Google → Meta)
4. 🎯 **Self-reported attribution** + UTM tracking for full picture
5. 🎯 **Test one variable at a time** (disciplined A/B testing)
6. 🎯 **Pause ads <0.45% CTR** immediately (protects account quality)
7. 🎯 **Contact LinkedIn rep monthly** for penetration reports
8. 🎯 **Document learnings** in playbook (institutional knowledge)

### Red Flags (Avoid These Mistakes)

1. ❌ **Title-based only targeting** (use skills + functions instead)
2. ❌ **Generic creative** (not native to LinkedIn)
3. ❌ **Lead-only optimization** (should optimize for SQLs/pipeline)
4. ❌ **No creative refresh** (ad fatigue kills performance after 14 days)
5. ❌ **Judging too early** (<90 days, <$10K spend)
6. ❌ **Audience expansion enabled** (dilutes targeting)
7. ❌ **No retargeting setup** (missing 40% of conversions)
8. ❌ **Ignoring frequency** (over-targeting causes burnout)
